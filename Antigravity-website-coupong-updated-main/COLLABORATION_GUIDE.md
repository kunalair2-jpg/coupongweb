# 🤝 How to Collaborate using VS Code Live Share

You have chosen **Live Share** to work with **kunalair2@gmail.com**. This is the best way for real-time pair programming!

---

## Step 1: Install the Extension 🧩

1.  Open **VS Code**.
2.  Click the **Extensions** icon on the left sidebar (it looks like 4 squares).
    *   *Shortcut:* `Ctrl+Shift+X`
3.  Search for: **`Live Share`**
4.  Install the **Live Share** extension by **Microsoft**.
    *   *(It usually has millions of downloads)*.
5.  **Reload** VS Code if prompted.

---

## Step 2: Start a Session 🚀

1.  Look at the **Bottom Status Bar** (blue/purple bar at the very bottom).
2.  Click the button that says **`Live Share`** (or `Share`).
    *   *Alternative:* Press `F1` or `Ctrl+Shift+P`, type `Live Share: Start`, and press Enter.
3.  You will be asked to **Sign In**.
    *   Choose **Sign in with GitHub** or **Microsoft**.
    *   Follow the browser prompts to authorize.

---

## Step 3: Invite Your Friend 📩

1.  Once the session starts, a notification will pop up:  
    **"Invitation link copied to clipboard!"**
2.  If you missed it, click the **Live Share icon** (arrow/circle) in the sidebar -> **Copy Invite Link**.
3.  **Send this link to `kunalair2@gmail.com`** (via email, WhatsApp, Slack, etc.).

---

## Step 4: What Kunal Needs to Do 👤

1.  Click the link you sent.
2.  It will open in their browser or VS Code.
3.  They just need to sign in (as Guest or with GitHub).
4.  **Boom!** They will appear in your editor, and you can see their cursor and type together.

> **Note:** Since your backend runs locally (`localhost:5000`), you might need to "Share Server" in the Live Share tab so Kunal can see the running app.
> *   Go to **Live Share tab** -> **Shared Servers** -> **Add Shared Server** -> Enter `5000`.
